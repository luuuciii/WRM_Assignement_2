2. The monthly data have a strong seasonal correlation. Normally this would be treated with stochastic models such as PAR and PARMA ( Periodic AR and Periodic ARMA). These models where not taught in class, so you are left with AR and ARMA. 
There are to my knowledge two possible solution to this problem, either of which is not wrong per-se, just leads to different results. As long as you justify your choices and show strong mathematical reasoning both will be taken into account as correct. Pursue just one of the two please. 

AVOID overfitting, and balance model parsimosiuness. If the distribution of values is stationary and the data are seasonal, that means that your AR or ARMA model should not be more than a 12th order (12th included). Removing linear trends before modelling is therefore fundamental. Also discuss the stationarity of the data before modelling.
